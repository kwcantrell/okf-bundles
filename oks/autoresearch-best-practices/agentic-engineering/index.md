# Agentic Engineering

AutoResearch is a concrete instance of a broader shift Andrej Karpathy has
described: coding is moving from typing lines yourself to orchestrating agents
that write them. Karpathy first named the loose end of that spectrum "vibe
coding," and has since argued that the disciplined end — where you design the
spec, review the diffs, and own the outcome — deserves its own name and its own
expertise: "agentic engineering." This area covers that framing and why the
[agent research loop](/oks/autoresearch-best-practices/loop/agent-research-loop.md)
needs engineering discipline wrapped around it rather than blind trust.

## Concepts

- [vibe coding to agentic engineering](/oks/autoresearch-best-practices/agentic-engineering/vibe-coding-to-agentic-engineering.md) — Karpathy's own arc: coining "vibe coding," describing his workflow flipping from mostly manual to mostly agent-driven, and proposing "agentic engineering" as the more accurate successor term.
- [engineering discipline](/oks/autoresearch-best-practices/agentic-engineering/engineering-discipline.md) — why orchestrating capable agents is engineering, not just prompting: spec design, diff review, eval design, and security oversight, with benchmark evidence for why that discipline is not optional.
- [karpathy guidelines](/oks/autoresearch-best-practices/agentic-engineering/karpathy-guidelines.md) — a widely-copied community CLAUDE.md distilled from Karpathy's observations about how LLMs fail at coding, framed as the operating discipline around an autonomous loop.
