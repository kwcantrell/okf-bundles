# Vibe Coding to Agentic Engineering

AutoResearch did not appear in a vacuum. It is one expression of a shift Andrej
Karpathy has been narrating publicly: the day-to-day act of writing software is
moving from a human typing the lines to a human orchestrating agents that type
them. The vocabulary for that shift is itself Karpathy's, and it has moved twice.

## Karpathy coined "vibe coding"

In a February 2025 post, Karpathy named a new mode of working with code that he
called "vibe coding" — leaning fully into the model's output, "embracing
exponentials," and largely forgetting that the code itself exists, on the premise
that LLM coding tools had gotten good enough to make that viable. The term is his
coinage, and it spread far beyond the original post to become the general label
for loosely-supervised, prompt-driven building.

## The workflow ratio flipped

Karpathy has also described, in his own words, how far his personal workflow
moved in a short span. He reported going from roughly 80% manual-plus-autocomplete
coding and 20% agents to roughly the inverse — about 80% agent-driven coding and
20% edits and touch-ups — over a matter of weeks as coding models improved. The
figures are his own description of his own practice, not a general measurement;
the point is the direction and the speed of the flip, from a person doing the
typing to a person steering agents that do it. That is the same inversion the
[agent research loop](/oks/autoresearch-best-practices/loop/agent-research-loop.md)
formalizes: the human stops hand-tuning and instead authors the instructions the
agent iterates under.

## "Agentic engineering" as the successor term

Roughly a year after the original post, Karpathy revisited "vibe coding" and
argued it had become the wrong name for the serious version of the practice. He
proposed "agentic engineering" instead: *agentic* because you are orchestrating
agents that write the code, and *engineering* to insist that there is genuine art
and science to doing it well — a real expertise and depth, not just talking to a
chatbot. In a later fireside framing he sharpened the distinction: vibe coding
*raises the floor*, letting far more people produce working software, while
agentic engineering *raises the ceiling*, rewarding the practitioners who bring
real skill to steering the agents. The two are not the same activity wearing
different names; they sit at opposite ends of how much discipline the human
supplies. What that disciplined end actually requires is the subject of
[engineering discipline](/oks/autoresearch-best-practices/agentic-engineering/engineering-discipline.md).
